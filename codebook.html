1 Overview

The American Local Elections Study (ALES) is a regular post-election survey of the residents of medium- and large-sized cities and counties in the United States, covering hundreds of local races between 2020 and 2025. Survey items have been grouped thematically, and are designed to capture residents’ preferences for both federal and urban policy, their experiences and satisfaction with public services, their evaluations of elected officials, and their vote choices for elections from county executive to president.

1.1 Variable Naming Conventions

Most survey items appear in multiple column variants. The base variable name (e.g., fedpol_build_wall) is extended with a suffix indicating the column type:

Suffix Meaning Example
_n Numeric, scaled 0–1 fedpol_build_wall_n
_n_r Numeric, reverse-coded, scaled 0–1 fedpol_build_wall_n_r
_oc Ordered categorical (factor) fedpol_build_wall_oc
_bin Binary indicator (0/1 integer) approve_mayor_bin

We use consistent abbreviations when constructing variable names:

Abbreviation Meaning
dem Democrat
rep Republican
inc Incumbent
countyexec County Executive
gov Governor
house U.S. Representative
pres President
sen U.S. Senator

For numeric (_n / _n_r) columns, values are scaled to the 0–1 interval where 0 represents the first response option and 1 represents the last. Reverse-coded items (_n_r) are flipped so that higher values consistently indicate the conservative or anti-regulation direction. The ordered categorical (_oc) columns are never reverse-coded.

1.2 Missingness Conventions

The dataset distinguishes three types of missing data:

Numeric columns (_n, _n_r, _bin):

Value Meaning
-1 Don’t Know – respondent saw the question and chose the DK option
-2 Skipped – respondent saw the question but left it blank
NA Not Asked – question was not in the respondent’s survey wave or not applicable
-3 “This office doesn’t exist in my city or town” (approval questions only)

Categorical columns (_oc):

Value Meaning
"Don't know" Respondent chose DK (only present as a factor level when DK was an option)
"Skipped" Respondent left the question blank
"This office doesn't exist in my city or town" Approval questions only
NA Not Asked – question not in wave or not applicable
