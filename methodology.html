Methodology

Learn about the American Local Elections Study's survey design, data collection methods, and variable documentation.

Overview

The American Local Elections Survey (ALES) is a regular post-election survey of residents in medium- and large-sized cities and counties across the United States. Our survey captures hundreds of local races and provides comprehensive data on voter preferences, political attitudes, and local governance.

Survey Administration & Sampling

All ALES surveys were fielded online through quota samples provided by PureSpectrum. Respondents were recruited from opt-in online panels and compensated for their participation. Respondents were targeted by ZIP code and county to ensure accurate geographic representation within each sample frame. This targeting strategy allowed us to survey residents in specific cities and counties where local elections were held. Surveys from 2020 through 2025 were quota sampled on key demographic characteristics (age, gender, race and ethnicity, and education) to ensure representative samples within each location. For surveys fielded 2020-2023, quota targets were derived from the 2018 American Community Survey (ACS) 5-year estimates. For surveys fielded in 2025 and 2026, quota targets were derived from the 2023 ACS 5-year estimates.

Survey Waves

ALES has conducted surveys following major election cycles from 2020 through 2025:

  • November 2020: 4,643 respondents across 28 sample frames
  • April 2021: 189 respondents across 1 sample frame
  • November 2021: 6,206 respondents across 32 sample frames
  • November 2022: 11,771 respondents across 60 sample frames
  • November 2023: 1,963 respondents across 10 sample frames
  • November 2025: 7,863 respondents across 34 sample frames

Variable Categories

Our comprehensive dataset includes variables organized into the following thematic categories:

Demographics

Age, sex, race, education, income, household composition, religion

Political Identity

Party identification, ideology, strength of partisanship

Policy Preferences

Federal and local policy positions, spending preferences, revenue preferences

Local Conditions

Grades for local services, crime perceptions, satisfaction ratings

Official Approval

Approval ratings for federal, state, and local officials

Vote Choice

Presidential, congressional, gubernatorial, and local election vote choice

Political Participation

Voter turnout, campaign activities, civic engagement

Candidate Knowledge

Perceived partisanship and ideology of candidates and officials

Data Structure

Variables follow consistent naming conventions with suffixes indicating their format:

  • _n: Numeric, scaled 0–1
  • _n_r: Numeric, reverse-coded, scaled 0–1
  • _oc: Ordered categorical (factor)
  • _bin: Binary indicator (0/1 integer)

Election Coverage

ALES respondents were surveyed about elections at multiple levels of government:

  • Presidential elections
  • U.S. Senate races
  • U.S. House races
  • Gubernatorial elections
  • Mayoral elections (both partisan and nonpartisan)
  • County executive races
  • Sheriff elections
  • Prosecutor/District Attorney elections
